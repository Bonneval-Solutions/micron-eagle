### FAQs — Pump & Motor

**How long will it take to repair my pump?**
Need to evaluate the unit first. A simple reseal can often be done the same day. More complex units may need more parts — depends on manufacturer lead times.

**My pump isn't putting out pressure.**
The correct statement would be 'my pump isn't putting out flow' — pressure is a result of a restricted flow. Could be loss of efficiency or a control/valve issue. Send in for evaluation.

**Do you keep units on the shelf?**
Yes, many units in stock. Also offer a service exchange programme — keep one spare on the shelf, repair/test/store the counterpart when it comes in so you always have a unit ready to go.

**Can you repair my obsolete unit?**
Keep a large amount of surplus stock. Older units no longer being produced — gives depth to repair or reconfigure units no longer on the market.

**Can we witness our pump being tested?**
Not standard, but can be accommodated. Please call to arrange.

## 5. HYDRAULIC CYLINDER & RAM REPAIR (Dedicated Page)

Micron Eagle repair shop can inspect, evaluate, repair and test hydraulic cylinders and rams. Experienced technicians determine if minor repair/repacking is required or if a new cylinder would be more economical.

**Repair Process:**
1. Disassemble the cylinder
2. Inspect and evaluate damages/issues
3. Quote repair or replacement before moving further
4. Replace worn or damaged components with high-quality OEM parts
5. Reassemble to factory specifications
6. Test to ensure it meets or exceeds factory standards
7. Provide detailed test report

**Additional Services:**
- Preventive maintenance services with customized maintenance schedules
- Fast turnaround times
- Satisfaction guarantee

### FAQs — Cylinders

**Can I repack a cylinder myself?**
Advise sending in for inspection and test prior to reinstalling. If packing has gone bad there could be more internal damage.

**My cylinder is drifting, what should I do?**
Can be a sign piston seals are damaged and leaking by. Remove for evaluation immediately — can be very dangerous if left in service.

**Do you keep units on the shelf?**
Don't stock cylinders (too many possibilities). Good connections with many cylinder suppliers and manufacturers. Can offer a service exchange programme.

**Can you repair my obsolete unit?**
Often easier to have a new cylinder manufactured if obsolete. Can measure up old cylinder and quote for a new one.

## 15. CONTACT PAGE — FAQs

**What causes piston pump failure?**
Four most common modes: oil contamination, fatigue from transient pressure spikes, blocked/restricted pump inlet, pump case over pressurization.

**What causes a hydraulic motor to fail?**
Particle, air and water contamination are leading causes (80-90% of failures). Faulty pumps, system breaches or temperature issues often cause both types of contamination.

**Are axial piston pumps reversible?**
Bent axis pumps come in fixed and variable displacement configurations but cannot be reversed without internal modification.

**When should I maintain my piston pump?**
Depends on application — review OEM manual for part lifespans. Regular and preventative maintenance increases lifespan and decreases overhauls.

**Why is my hydraulic unit pulsating?**
Many factors: abnormal inlet conditions, fixed outlet plumbing, undersized bypass hose, non-working regulating valve, stuck/damaged pump valves.
